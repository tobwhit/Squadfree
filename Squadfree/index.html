The Problem

Human Trafficking is a global issue and is the second most profitable transnational crime after drug trafficking. In the United States alone, it is estimated to be a $32 billion per year industry. Most cases of human trafficking are unidentified and traumatize their victims. It is a form of modern-day slavery where individuals are traded for exploitation or commercial gain.

Children as Victims

Children account for half of the victims of human trafficking. In fact, the average age that a young person becomes involved in sex trafficking is 12 years old.

Database

In recent years, efforts have been made in the Utah area to create a human trafficking database to track and identify potential cases of trafficking. The Utah Trafficking in Persons (UTIP) Task Force has been leading the charge on this initiative, working with law enforcement agencies and community organizations to develop a comprehensive database that includes information on suspected trafficking cases, victims, and perpetrators. This database is intended to be a useful tool for law enforcement and other agencies in identifying and investigating potential cases of trafficking, as well as for outreach and education efforts. While the database is still in its early stages, it has the potential to be an important resource in the ongoing fight against human trafficking in the Utah area.
